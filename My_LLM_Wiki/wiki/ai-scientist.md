A concept and technology known as an [[AI Scientist]] refers to an [[Artificial Intelligence]] system capable of autonomously conducting [[scientific research]] from start to finish, including topic selection, experimentation, and [[paper writing]].

According to a post dated April 8, 2026, an interesting paper was published in [[Nature]] in March 2026 detailing an [[AI scientist]] that manages the entire research process.

This functionality was developed by [[Sakana AI]], a Japanese [[AI]] company. The [[AI Scientist]] works by:
1.  **Proposing research topics**: The system suggests potential areas of investigation.
2.  **Novelty detection**: It then verifies if the proposed topic possesses sufficient [[novelty]] when compared to existing research.
3.  **Topic selection**: Based on the novelty check, a research topic is selected.
4.  **Conducting research**: The [[AI]] directly performs the necessary research and experiments for the chosen topic.
5.  **Paper writing**: Finally, it writes the [[scientific paper]] based on its findings.

A paper produced and authored by this [[AI scientist]] successfully passed the [[peer review]] process for the [[International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR)]], a globally renowned academic conference in [[Artificial Intelligence]].

These achievements highlight [[AI]]'s growing capability to act, to a significant extent, as an [[independent researcher]].
