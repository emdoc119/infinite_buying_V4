## Overview

This note synthesizes observations and a methodology for predicting market corrections in the [[KOSPI Index]], initially based on data from [[KB Securities]] and further refined through the application of [[Bollinger Bands]]. The author emphasizes the importance of reviewing past corrections to better prepare for future market movements.

## Initial Observation: Divergence and KOSPI Corrections

Data from [[KB Securities]] suggests a correlation between the [[KOSPI Index]] and its [[50-day Moving Average]] ([[50-day MA]]) divergence. Historically, market corrections tend to occur when the divergence between the [[KOSPI Index]] and its [[50-day MA]] widens to **130%**.

**Observed Instances of Correction at 130% Divergence:**
*   November of last year
*   March, May, and June of this year

These past events consistently showed that when the [[Divergence]] reached 130%, a [[Market Correction]] followed.

## Challenge with Divergence Calculation

While the 130% [[Divergence]] rule proved effective, calculating the divergence itself is noted as not straightforward. This difficulty prompted the search for an easier and equally effective indicator.

## Solution: Applying Bollinger Bands

The idea arose to utilize [[Bollinger Bands]] as an alternative to the complex divergence calculation. By applying [[Bollinger Bands]] with specific parameters, a remarkably accurate correlation was found:

*   **Parameters:** [[50-day Moving Average]] with **2 standard deviations**.

When these [[Bollinger Bands]] parameters were applied to historical data, they effectively mirrored the correction signals previously identified by the 130% [[Divergence]]. The author found this method "matched wonderfully" and plans to use it for future [[Technical Analysis]] and [[Market Correction]] predictions.

## Conclusion

By combining insights from [[KB Securities]] data regarding [[KOSPI Index]] [[Divergence]] and the practical application of [[Bollinger Bands]] (50-day MA, 2 standard deviations), a robust and user-friendly method for anticipating [[Market Correction]]s has been identified. This approach aims to provide a clearer signal for market participants to respond effectively to upcoming adjustments.
